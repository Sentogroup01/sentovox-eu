Practical information

Everything else you need to know

Details on target audience, timing, data privacy, security, support, and more.

The study is designed for surveying visitors of your cultural venue - people who have been to your cultural venue within the past 12 months.

It is also possible to include non-visitors (never been). The intelligent survey logic identifies each profile and presents only relevant questions.

Questionnaires are available in German, English, French, Italian, Dutch, and Spanish. You choose which languages to offer. New languages are added regularly - missing one? Let us know.

SentoVox is designed for any type of cultural venue - small or large, rural or urban, regardless of collection or offer.

Discover the 130+ cultural venues that have already participated →

The data collection period can run year-round or within a timeframe you select. Standard plan venues can conduct the study during 6 months; Premium and Network cultural venues have 12 months.

For Network cultural venues, there are two fixed reporting points to choose from: June or December. All cultural venues have ongoing access to their interactive dashboard throughout the study period.

SentoVox does not automatically renew memberships. After each research period, you will be contacted to offer the possibility to continue or upgrade your participation.

Each cultural venue receives a personal survey link and QR code, plus personalised, ready-to-use communication materials for digital channels (newsletter, website, social media) and physical touchpoints (posters, flyers). The base package includes digital materials; printed materials are available as an option.

The survey is fully responsive (smartphone, tablet, laptop, desktop). Live interviews via cultural venue staff or volunteers are also possible. A simplified paper version is available.

For former visitors or non-visitors, dedicated materials are provided for distribution through the city, partner institutions, and social media channels.

To support cultural venues reaching enough respondents, incentives are provided by the SentoVox study. For each completed survey, 1m² of rain forest will be saved through the organisation World Land Trust. Next to that, participants have a chance of winning attractive prizes. The incentives are fully organized by the SentoVox research team and are included in the cost of the participation. All participating venues, regardless of plan, contribute to this environmental initiative — one square metre saved per completed survey.

All systems are secured according to current standards:

  • Secure servers with HTTPS / SSL encryption
  • Continuous server backups
  • Anti-BOT measures

Quality control measures ensure data integrity: consistency checks through control questions, detection of 'speeders', and anonymised IP address checks.

The study is conducted in full compliance with the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The survey is completely anonymous - no personal data of respondents is collected or stored.

Prize winners are determined by a random code system. Selected winners see a unique code after submission and contact the research team voluntarily. Contact details are deleted immediately afterward.

Your research results are the private property of your cultural venue. They are shared only with your designated contact person(s). All benchmark analyses use anonymised data.

SentoVox is designed to be easy to implement. Step-by-step plans and short explanatory videos guide you through the complete study.

Premium and Network cultural venues have access to extra support via a professional helpdesk with ticket tracking.

Cultural venue visitors can also reach the helpdesk via the "Help" button in the survey.

Groups of cultural venues enjoy extra benefits including tailored pricing, group participation, group reports, group-level questions, and a shared structure on the platform.

Network groups additionally receive individual reports per location, benchmarking with group-wide results, and raw data at group level.

The study is offered to cultural venues completely independently. There is no external funding, which means results and data are completely confidential and analysis is conducted without any external influence. You decide if and how you want to publish your local results.
